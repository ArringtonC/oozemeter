# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Everyday consumers, homebuyers, families tracking expenses, and students who feel the economy is "good" or "bad" but don't know why. Secondary: investors, journalists, realtors. Context: a quick daily check, mostly on phones, the way people check the weather. The job to be done: "How stressed is the U.S. economy today, and what's driving it?" answered in under five seconds.

## Product Purpose

OOZEMeter aggregates trusted public economic data (FRED, BLS, AAA, Freddie Mac, NY Fed) into one visual score, the Ooze Level, rendered as a lab jar of green ooze inside a fictional government containment facility. Business model: ad-supported media network. Success = daily returning visitors, multiple pages per session, shareable moments, search traffic to indicator pages.

## Brand Personality

"Bloomberg meets Area 51." A classified laboratory that accidentally makes economics understandable. Three words: credible, playful, alive. Influences: Aperture Science (Portal), SCP Foundation, Fallout terminals, with the polish of Linear and the typography discipline of Stripe. The Jar is the mascot; laboratory micro-copy carries the humor ("Fresh specimen collected", "Fresh leaks detected", "Please do not tap the glass").

## Anti-references

- Generic finance dashboards and CNBC-style chart walls (data density over storytelling).
- Childish or cartoon mascot treatments; the humor is deadpan-institutional, never goofy.
- Doom-mongering "recession predictor" framing; this measures stress, it does not prophesy.
- Perpetual ambient animation. Motion is staged: fill, then rest. Constant movement is noise.

## Design Principles

- The Jar is the main character; every screen orbits the specimen.
- Data first, not fear: every score explainable, weights published, sources linked.
- Delight over density: fewer charts, more storytelling; interactions teach (hover a pipe, understand a driver).
- Stillness is the default state; motion means something changed.
- Playful without trivializing financial hardship.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Respect prefers-reduced-motion (boot and ambient motion disabled). Keyboard operability for nodes, incidents, slider. Mobile-first: touch targets 44px+, no horizontal scroll, readable at small sizes. Alert states never rely on color alone (tier labels accompany every color change).
